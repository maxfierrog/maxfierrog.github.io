R&D group GamesCrafters#

Berkeley, CA – Jan 2023 to now

We research computational methods for strongly solving abstract strategy games (and make the solution sets available through a few types of interfaces). I am personally interested in the computational modelling of coalitional games, and in developing infrastructure to solve this type of game generically across player count.

Things I am working on…
  • Creating a re-architected version of GamesmanClassic, the group’s seminal project, in Rust.
  • Modelling N-player transferrable-utility coalitional games in cases of complete information and turn-based play with the objective of computing their strong solution sets.
  • An ACID-compliant and more performant re-implementation of the database engine we use to store our solution sets, which will provide the needed infrastructure to implement…
    • A lookup table based database compression scheme.
    • Parallelized solving algorithms.

Finished projects I have contributed to…
  • Solving Five Field Kono (you can play against the solution here).
  • Outreach during CalDay 2023!
