Abstract

Linear algebra, signal processing, and machine learning are (one of many groups of) topics that enjoy a beautiful relationship protected by a nutshell of mathematics. Inside we can find a kernel of perspectives which are simply a pleasure to entertain. Here, I provide the linear-operator perspective into systems of many dimensions, characterizing properties like time-invariance and causality via tensor representations. I finish with an original perspective that connects the convolution kernel to the reproducing kernel.

Background

This section is no more than a refresher on select topics. I will not make an attempt to explain them completely. If this is what you need, I recommend you explore these (completely free) resources:

  1. Vector spaces. Linear Algebra Done Right by Axler.
  2. Signals and Systems. Signals & Systems: Theory and Applications by Ulaby and Yagle.
  3. Kernel methods. Foundations of Machine Learning by Mohri, Rostamizadeh, and Talwalkar.
